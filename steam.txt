[h1]Zed's Item Tiers[/h1]

Tired of the same loot everywhere and losing the thrill of exploration? [b]Zed's Item Tiers[/b] adds a [b]probability-based rarity system[/b] to Project Zomboid: almost any item can roll as [b]Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, or Legendary[/b] when it enters the world. Higher tiers are genuinely stronger—lighter bags, deadlier weapons, tougher clothes—so scavenging and crafting stay interesting instead of flat. [b]New highlight:[/b] tiered clothing now has active [b]Thermoregulation[/b] behavior, not just static stats.

[h2]How it works[/h2]
[list]
[*]Tiers are assigned when items spawn or appear in containers; [b]Common[/b] items behave like vanilla (no tier bonuses).
[*]Each tier has a [b]default spawn weight[/b] (see Tiers below). You can tune probabilities in the mod’s Lua if you want a stingier or more generous economy.
[*][b]Tooltips[/b] show the item’s tier and the bonuses that apply to that item type, so you can compare loot at a glance.
[/list]

[h2]Tiers (default chances)[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]Common[/b] — 80% — White (no bonuses)
[*][b]Uncommon[/b] — 16% — Green
[*][b]Rare[/b] — 3.2% — Blue
[*][b]Epic[/b] — 0.64% — Purple
[*][b]Legendary[/b] — 0.16% — Gold
[/list]

[h2]New: Thermoregulation (clothing)</h2>
[b]Thermoregulation is now an active mechanic.[/b] On insulated clothing, tier bonuses dynamically adapt insulation while worn:
[list]
[*]All clothing from [b]Rare tier and above[/b] gets Thermoregulation.
[*]If covered body parts run [b]hot[/b], insulation shifts [b]down[/b] (cooling support).
[*]If covered body parts run [b]cold[/b], insulation shifts [b]up[/b] (warming support).
[*]The shift range is tied to the item's [b]Thermoregulation bonus[/b], so higher-tier clothing adapts more.
[*]Thermoregulated clothing also [b]self-dries faster[/b] when wet.
[/list]

[h2]Bonuses by item type[/h2]
Bonuses only apply where the item actually supports the stat. [b]Thermoregulation[/b] is one of the headline systems now. In practice:

[list]
[*][b]Weapons:[/b] damage, crit, handling-style stats where applicable; [b]weapon weight[/b] needs the optional Java path (see ZombieBuddy).
[*][b]Clothing:[/b] bite/scratch (and related) defense, run speed, vision and hearing modifiers where relevant, plus [b]thermoregulation[/b] on insulated gear: while worn, insulation nudges toward what your covered body parts need (hotter → less insulation, colder → more), and tiered clothes with thermoregulation [b]dry faster[/b] when wet.
[*][b]Containers:[/b] capacity, encumbrance reduction, max item size where supported.
[*][b]Drainables:[/b] fluid capacity where the item uses a fluid container.
[*][b]Literature:[/b] mood-related stats and reading speed where the book supports them.
[*][b]Flashlights / battery gear:[/b] lower drain where hooked.
[*][b]Food:[/b] improved hunger reduction on higher tiers where the item is food.
[*][b]Legendary style bonus:[/b] all [b]worn Legendary[/b] items provide a small mood/stress boost, so yes, your cool bellybutton piercing finally has gameplay value.
[/list]

[h2]Crafting[/h2]
Crafted output tier follows your [b]ingredient tiers[/b] in a [b]Factorio-style[/b] way: the result is never worse than the “floor” set by your best ingredient tier, mixed inputs influence the roll, and [b]relevant crafting skill[/b] can slightly improve (or worsen) the outcome. Recycling good components into better gear is part of the loop.

[h2]Optional: ZombieBuddy[/h2]
[url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3619862853][b]ZombieBuddy[/b][/url] enables [b]Java-side[/b] bonuses that pure Lua cannot apply cleanly—most notably [b]weapon weight[/b]. The mod is fully playable without it; you just skip those Java-only perks.
